## Mopping: Roborock Wins Decisively

This is the biggest differentiator. Roborock's S8 MaxV Ultra has a vibrating mopping system (VibraRise) that:
- Lifts the mop pad when carpet is detected
- Applies sonic vibration for actual scrubbing
- Dock washes and dries mop pads with hot water

Roomba Combo j9+ added mopping, but it's a bolt-on compared to Roborock's purpose-built system.

## The Dock Comparison

This single comparison point has shifted a lot of buying decisions:

| Feature | Roomba Clean Base | Roborock Ultra Dock |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Auto-empty dust bin | Yes | Yes |
| Mop washing | No | Yes (hot water) |
| Mop drying | No | Yes (hot air) |
| Water refilling | No | Yes (auto) |
| Solution dispensing | No | Yes |

Roborock's dock is a full cleaning station. Roomba's empties the dust bin.

## Navigation: Different Approaches

Both use LiDAR + camera:
- **Roomba:** More cautious. Fewer collisions, slower overall. Excellent pet waste avoidance.
- **Roborock:** More efficient pathing. Faster completion, occasional furniture bumps. Better 3D mapping in the app.

## Where Roomba Still Wins

**Long-term support.** iRobot has sold robot vacuums since 2002. Replacement parts are at retail stores. Customer support is mature. Roborock is building this track record but isn't there yet.

**Simplicity.** Roomba's app is more approachable for non-technical users. Roborock rewards tinkerers.

## Price Reality

- Roomba j9+ with Clean Base: ~$799-$899
- Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra: ~$1,399-$1,599

Roborock costs more at the flagship level, but the dock does significantly more. At mid-range (Roborock S8 vs Roomba j7+), Roborock is often cheaper with more features.

## Bottom Line

Roborock offers more features and innovation at every price point. Roomba's ecosystem maturity and brand trust still matter for less tech-forward buyers.
