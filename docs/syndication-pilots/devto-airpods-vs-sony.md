## Sound Quality: Sony Takes It

Sony's Dynamic Driver X delivers wider frequency response and richer bass. The difference is clear on complex tracks — more texture in strings, more authority in bass, wider soundstage.

AirPods Pro 2 aren't bad — Apple's computational audio processing delivers clean, balanced sound. But head-to-head, audiophiles consistently rank Sony higher.

## Noise Cancellation: Different Philosophies

**Sony** blocks more raw noise, especially at low frequencies. Best-in-class for planes and commutes.

**Apple** manages noise more intelligently. Adaptive Transparency reduces loud sounds in real-time while keeping you situationally aware. Conversation Awareness auto-pauses when you talk.

Both are top-tier — they just optimize for different things.

## The Ecosystem Question

This is where most buying decisions actually happen:

- **iPhone + iPad + Mac user?** → AirPods Pro 2. Seamless switching, Spatial Audio with head tracking, Find My with Precision Finding.
- **Android user?** → Sony WF-1000XM5. Multipoint connection, LDAC high-res codec, full EQ customization via Sony app.
- **Mixed devices?** → Sony edges it with broader compatibility.

## The Bottom Line

There's no wrong choice. The real question is your ecosystem, not the earbuds themselves.
