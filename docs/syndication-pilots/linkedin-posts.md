## Post 4: The "vs" Search Economy (Thought Leadership)

Something interesting about search behavior:

"Best earbuds 2026" → 40,000 monthly searches
"AirPods Pro 2 vs Sony WF-1000XM5" → 50,000+ monthly searches

The specific comparison query gets MORE traffic than the generic "best" query.

This pattern repeats across every category we track:

"Best robot vacuum" → 25K/mo
"Roomba vs Roborock" → 30K+/mo

"Best coffee machine" → 15K/mo
"Nespresso vs Keurig" → 15K+/mo

People don't want a listicle of the "10 best" products. They want to know: should I get THIS one or THAT one?
