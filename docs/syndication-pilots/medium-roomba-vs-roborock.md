# Roomba vs Roborock: The Definitive Robot Vacuum Comparison for 2026

*Originally published on [aversusb.net](https://www.aversusb.net/roomba-vs-roborock). Read the full data-driven comparison with specs, ratings, and community verdicts there.*

---

"Should I get a Roomba or a Roborock?" is one of the most searched product comparisons on the internet — over 30,000 people ask this question every month. After years of iRobot's dominance, Roborock has emerged as the most credible challenger. Here's how they actually stack up.

## The Short Answer

**Choose Roomba** if you want a mature ecosystem with proven navigation, excellent customer support, and the brand name that defined robot vacuums. **Choose Roborock** if you want more features per dollar — better mopping, stronger suction, and a self-empty dock that does more for less.

## Cleaning Performance

### Suction Power

Roborock's flagship S8 MaxV Ultra delivers up to 10,000Pa of suction — significantly more than the Roomba j9+'s estimated 3,000-4,000Pa. In practice, both handle daily debris well on hard floors, but the difference shows on carpets:

- **Deep carpet cleaning:** Roborock extracts more embedded dirt and pet hair
- **Hard floor efficiency:** Both perform similarly; Roomba's rubber extractors handle hair tangles better
- **Edge cleaning:** Roomba's side brush design reaches corners more effectively

### Mopping

This is where Roborock pulls decisively ahead. The S8 MaxV Ultra has a vibrating mopping system (VibraRise) that lifts the mop pad when it detects carpet and applies sonic vibration for scrubbing.

The Roomba Combo j9+ added mopping capability, but it's a bolt-on solution compared to Roborock's purpose-built system. Roborock mops harder, lifts the pad automatically, and the dock washes and dries the mop pads with hot water.

**Verdict:** Roborock wins mopping decisively. Roomba is competitive on vacuuming alone.

## Navigation and Obstacle Avoidance

Both use LiDAR + camera-based navigation, but their approaches differ:

- **Roomba j9+:** PrecisionVision Navigation identifies and avoids objects (shoes, cables, pet waste) with high accuracy. iRobot has years of refinement here, and it shows.
- **Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra:** Reactive 3D structured light + RGB camera. Slightly faster mapping, handles complex floor plans well, and the Roborock app gives you granular room-by-room control.

In head-to-head navigation tests, Roomba tends to be more cautious (fewer collisions but slower), while Roborock is more efficient (faster cleaning paths but occasionally bumps furniture).

**Verdict:** Tie. Roomba is more cautious; Roborock is faster. Both navigate effectively.

## Self-Empty Dock Comparison

| Feature | Roomba j9+ Clean Base | Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra Dock |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Auto-empty dust bin | Yes | Yes |
| Mop washing | No | Yes (hot water) |
| Mop drying | No | Yes (hot air) |
| Water refilling | No | Yes (auto from tank) |
| Solution dispensing | No | Yes |
| Self-cleaning | Basic | Comprehensive |

Roborock's dock is essentially a full cleaning station. Roomba's is a dust bin emptier. This single comparison point has shifted a lot of buying decisions toward Roborock.

## App Experience

Both apps are well-designed, but Roborock gives you more control:

- **Room-specific suction/water levels:** Roborock lets you set different cleaning intensities per room
- **No-go zones and invisible walls:** Both support this; Roborock's implementation is more flexible
- **Scheduling:** Both offer per-room scheduling; Roborock adds more granular time controls
- **3D mapping:** Roborock's 3D floor plan view is more detailed

Roomba's app is simpler and more approachable for non-technical users. Roborock's app rewards users who want to fine-tune every setting.

## Maintenance and Longevity

Roomba has a significant edge in parts availability and long-term support. iRobot has been selling robot vacuums since 2002, and replacement parts (brushes, filters, batteries) are widely available at retail stores.

Roborock parts are available but primarily through Amazon and the Roborock store. Long-term reliability data is still building compared to iRobot's 20+ year track record.

## Price

- **Roomba j9+ with Clean Base:** ~$799-$899
- **Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra:** ~$1,399-$1,599

Roborock costs more at the top end, but you're getting a vastly more capable dock. If you compare vacuum-only models (Roborock S8 vs Roomba j7+), Roborock is often cheaper while offering more features.

## The Bottom Line

| Priority | Best Choice |
|----------|-------------|
| Best mopping | Roborock |
| Best all-in-one dock | Roborock |
| Best brand support & longevity | Roomba |
| Best value at mid-range | Roborock |
| Best for simple setup | Roomba |
| Best for pet owners | Roomba (obstacle avoidance for pet waste) |
| Best for large homes | Roborock (faster, bigger battery) |

The market has shifted. Roborock offers more features and innovation at every price point, but Roomba's ecosystem maturity, customer support, and brand trust still matter — especially for less tech-savvy buyers.
